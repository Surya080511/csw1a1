"S = vit + 1/2 at2: The Equation of Linear Motion. "

The formula S = vit + 1/2 at2 describes the motion of an object under constant acceleration. Here :

  • S represents the displacement,
  • vi is the initial velocity,
  • t is the time,
  • a is the acceleration, and
  • 1/2 at2 is the additional displacement due to acceleration

An object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an External force.
Note : This equation was originally written in 1905.
